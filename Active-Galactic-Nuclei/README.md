# Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN)

This mini-project demonstrates the use of the Monte Carlo method to simulate the energy distribution of gamma-ray photons emitted by an AGN (Active Galactic Nucleus), which follows the distribution:

$F(E) = A \cdot E^{\ - \gamma}$  

Where:

* $E$ is the photon energy
* $\gamma$ is the spectral index, (we suppose $\gamma = 1.8$)

The photon energy ranges from $E_{\text{min}} = 10$ to $E_{\text{max}} = 100$. After defining this distribution, we simulate the response of a photon energy detector with an energy resolution of 10% and superimpose the two resulting histograms.
